# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Personal power users who curate their own reading list obsessively. They save tens of links per week from the browser and mobile, then return to browse, search, and recall them. Their context: mid-task, often distracted, already inside another workflow (browsing, reading, sharing). The job is speed — save without friction, find without hunting.

## Product Purpose

Amber is a cross-platform link-saving tool (Chrome extension + Flutter mobile) that lets users capture, organize, and revisit URLs. It exists because the default browser bookmarks are a graveyard: hard to search, impossible to share intent with your future self. Success looks like: a link saved in under 3 seconds, found in under 5.

## Brand Personality

Focused, calm, reliable. A tool that knows exactly what it is and doesn't apologize for it. No onboarding lectures, no celebration animations. It's there when you need it, invisible when you don't.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard**: Blue-and-white sidebar grids where every tool looks identical. Amber has a distinct visual identity — dark-first, amber-accented, opinionated.
- **Notion / productivity overload**: Feature-dense, text-everywhere, database-brain aesthetic. Amber is a capture tool, not a second brain. Complexity is the enemy.

## Design Principles

1. **Speed is the primary courtesy.** Every interaction should feel instant. Saving takes priority over organizing. Never add a step between intent and action.
2. **Calm over novelty.** No unnecessary motion, no decorative complexity. Trust is built through consistency and restraint. Motion, when present, confirms — it doesn't entertain.
3. **Tool confidence.** The UI has an opinion. It doesn't surface every option at once. Defaults are correct; advanced options exist but stay out of the way.
4. **Signal over chrome.** Every visual element carries information or creates space for it. Ornament is friction. The amber accent earns its place by pointing to the one thing that matters on each screen.
5. **One focus per surface.** Popup: save. New tab: browse and find. Mobile: capture and recall. Each surface has one primary task. It never competes with itself.
6. **Flat by default.** Every surface is flat at rest. Depth is earned by interaction state, not decoration. No shadow on a surface that isn't responding to user intent. Tonal layering (floor → surface → elevated) creates all structural hierarchy; shadows confirm state.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AAA: body text ≥ 7:1 contrast, large text (≥18px or bold ≥14px) ≥ 4.5:1. Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Keyboard navigable throughout. Both dark and light themes maintained at AAA standard. Screen reader friendly: semantic HTML, ARIA labels on interactive elements, state announcements on async operations.
